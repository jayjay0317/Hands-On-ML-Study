# Chapter 1: The Machine Learning Landscape
## 💡 Key Takeaways
Feature Engineering is crucial: I learned that feeding raw data into a model is rarely effective. Creating meaningful new variables from existing ones (Feature Extraction) is often more impactful than tuning the algorithm itself.

System-level thinking & Deployment: I realized that building a model is only half the battle. Understanding Batch vs. Online learning and real-world deployment challenges shifted my perspective. Issues like models being too slow for real-time use, failing to scale, or becoming outdated without frequent updates taught me to consider the entire ML lifecycle and computing costs, not just predictive accuracy.

## 🧠 Self-Reflection & Insights
One of the most interesting parts of this chapter was the concept of Reinforcement Learning. It immediately reminded me of studying Operant Conditioning for the MCAT Psychology section. 

Seeing how B.F. Skinner's theory of shaping biological behaviour through rewards and punishments is mathematically applied to train algorithms was a great realization. It helped me see machine learning not just as abstract equations, but as a digital application of behavioural psychology. This connection actually makes the mathematical concepts much more approachable to me.
